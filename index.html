Simple

Creamy Chicken & Rice Casserole

⏱ 45 min · 🍽 Serves 6 · 🥄 One pan

The kind of dish that filled the house with warmth on rainy Tuesdays. Tender chicken, soft rice, and a creamy blanket of cheese.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless chicken thighs
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 ½ cups chicken broth
  • 1 can cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar
  • Salt, pepper, paprika, butter

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Butter a 9×13 baking dish.
  2. Spread rice and onion in the dish. Pour broth and soup over, stir gently.
  3. Season chicken with salt, pepper, paprika. Place on top.
  4. Cover with foil. Bake 40 minutes.
  5. Uncover, sprinkle cheese, bake 5 more minutes until golden.
  6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve warm with peas or green beans.

Grandma's tip: A pinch of dried thyme makes the kitchen smell like Sunday.

Simple

Classic Tomato Soup & Grilled Cheese

⏱ 25 min · 🍽 Serves 4 · 🥄 Stovetop

Velvety tomato soup with a buttery, golden grilled cheese — the ultimate after-school memory.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans whole peeled tomatoes
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 cup whole milk or cream
  • 1 tsp sugar, salt, pepper
  • Sourdough bread + sharp cheddar

Instructions

  1. Melt butter, soften onion 8 minutes.
  2. Add tomatoes (with juice), sugar, salt. Simmer 15 minutes.
  3. Blend smooth. Stir in milk, warm gently.
  4. Butter bread, layer cheese, grill until golden on both sides.
  5. Serve hot with a sprinkle of black pepper.
Simple

Old-Fashioned Apple Crumble

⏱ 50 min · 🍽 Serves 8 · 🥄 One dish

Cinnamon apples under a buttery oat topping. Best with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Ingredients

  • 6 apples, peeled and sliced
  • ¼ cup brown sugar, 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • ¾ cup flour, ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup cold butter, cubed
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Toss apples with brown sugar and cinnamon, place in baking dish.
  3. Mix oats, flour, sugar, salt. Cut in butter until crumbly.
  4. Sprinkle topping over apples.
  5. Bake 35–40 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  6. Cool 10 minutes. Serve warm with ice cream.
Sunday Special

Slow-Braised Sunday Pot Roast

⏱ 4 hours · 🍽 Serves 8 · 🥄 Dutch oven

Fork-tender beef, root vegetables, and a deep gravy that's been on the stove since church let out.

Ingredients

  • 4 lb chuck roast
  • 3 tbsp flour, salt, pepper
  • 2 tbsp oil + 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 4 carrots, 4 celery ribs, chopped
  • 6 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup red wine (or extra broth)
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 2 bay leaves, 4 thyme sprigs, 2 rosemary sprigs
  • 1.5 lb baby potatoes, halved

Instructions

  1. Pat roast dry. Season heavily, dust with flour.
  2. Sear in hot oil + butter until deep brown, all sides. Remove.
  3. Sauté onion, carrot, celery 6 minutes. Add garlic, tomato paste, cook 2 minutes.
  4. Deglaze with wine, scrape brown bits. Add broth and herbs.
  5. Return roast, cover, braise at 325°F for 2.5 hours.
  6. Add potatoes, braise 1 more hour until everything is tender.
  7. Rest meat 15 minutes. Reduce sauce on stovetop if needed.
  8. Slice against the grain. Spoon sauce over everything.

Grandma's tip: Make it a day ahead — the flavor deepens overnight.

From Scratch

Homemade Chicken Pot Pie

⏱ 2 hours · 🍽 Serves 6 · 🥄 Pie dish

Flaky double crust, creamy filling, and the kind of steam that fogs up the kitchen window.

Crust

  • 2 ½ cups flour, 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 cup very cold butter, cubed
  • 6–8 tbsp ice water

Filling

  • 1 lb cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup peas, 1 cup diced carrots, 1 cup diced potato
  • ½ cup butter, ½ cup flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth, 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 onion diced, 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp thyme, salt, pepper
  • 1 egg + 1 tbsp milk (egg wash)

Instructions

  1. Pulse flour, salt, sugar. Cut in butter until pea-size.
  2. Add ice water 1 tbsp at a time until dough comes together. Chill 1 hour.
  3. Boil potatoes and carrots 5 minutes, drain.
  4. Sauté onion in butter, add garlic, then flour. Cook 1 minute.
  5. Whisk in broth and milk. Simmer until thick.
  6. Stir in chicken, peas, potatoes, carrots, thyme. Cool slightly.
  7. Roll bottom crust into pie dish. Fill, top with second crust, crimp edges.
  8. Brush with egg wash, cut steam vents.
  9. Bake at 400°F for 35–40 minutes until golden.
  10. Rest 15 minutes — the filling needs to settle.
Showstopper

Layered Banana Pudding Trifle

⏱ 1 hr + chill · 🍽 Serves 10 · 🥄 Trifle dish

Vanilla custard from scratch, ripe bananas, vanilla wafers, and clouds of whipped cream.

Custard

  • 3 cups whole milk
  • ¾ cup sugar, ¼ cup cornstarch, ¼ tsp salt
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 2 tbsp butter, 2 tsp vanilla

Layers

  • 1 box vanilla wafers
  • 5 ripe bananas, sliced
  • 2 cups heavy cream + 3 tbsp powdered sugar

Instructions

  1. Whisk sugar, cornstarch, salt in saucepan. Add milk gradually.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thick (8 min).
  3. Temper yolks: whisk a cup of hot milk into yolks, then return to pan.
  4. Cook 2 more minutes. Off heat, stir in butter and vanilla.
  5. Cover surface with plastic wrap, chill 1 hour.
  6. Whip cream with sugar to soft peaks.
  7. In trifle dish: wafers → bananas → custard → cream. Repeat 3 layers.
  8. Top with crushed wafers. Chill at least 4 hours before serving.

Grandma's tip: Don't skip the chill — the wafers turn into soft cake.
