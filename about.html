Our Story

A family kitchen, opened to the world.

Where It All Began

Grandma's Kitchen started at a worn pine table in Pittsburgh, with a notebook full of handwritten recipes passed down through four generations. We noticed something — recipes online had become too fast, too fancy, and too forgetful of the dishes that actually fed families for decades.

So we slowed it down. We pulled out the casserole dish, dusted off the pot roast pan, and started filming the way Grandma actually cooked: patiently, with big print on the recipe card and time enough to chat between steps.

What We Believe

  • Food should be familiar. The best meals are the ones you already remember.
  • Recipes should be readable. No squinting, no scrolling through life stories — just the recipe.
  • Cooking should be calm. A slow video is a kind one.
  • Comfort feeds more than hunger. A casserole is also a hug.

The People in the Kitchen

Our small team is made up of home cooks, video editors, and recipe testers — all of whom grew up in kitchens that smelled of onions softening in butter. We test every recipe at least three times before sharing it, and we read every email that comes through our contact page.
